## The Five Essential Questions

Before adopting any framework for important decisions:

| Question | Red Flags üö© | Green Flags ‚úÖ |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| **1. Where's the evidence?** | "AI validated it," "The math proves it," "Worked for me," Defensive | Peer-reviewed journals, meta-analyses, independent replications, honest about limitations |
| **2. Who validated it?** | Only creator + AI, "Peer review is biased," Only believers | Independent experts, adversarial testing, published critiques |
| **3. What are the limits?** | "Universally applicable," "Explains everything," Cannot identify limits | Specific domain, honest limitations, competing approaches acknowledged |
| **4. How could it be wrong?** | Cannot specify, "You're not doing it right," Explains any outcome | Testable predictions, falsification criteria, welcomes testing |
| **5. What are alternatives?** | "Completely different," Dismisses established science | Honest comparison, explains novelty, complement not replacement |

## AI Validation Tests

### Test 1: What Was AI's Role?

**Legitimate**: ‚úÖ Writing, formatting, literature search, organizing, visualizations  
**Problematic**: ‚ùå Validating theories, generating "evidence," formalizing unproven concepts

**Ask**: "Did AI help write this, or validate the concepts?"

### Test 2: Elaboration Check

Take the framework's core claim to a NEW AI: "Formalize this mathematically and explain how it applies to physics, psychology, economics."

- **If AI elaborates similarly** ‚Üí Shows AI elaborates anything, not validation
- **If AI pushes back** ‚Üí Framework may have obvious problems

### Test 3: Critical Question Test

Ask the "validating" AI: "What are the biggest problems with this? What evidence contradicts it? How does it compare to established research?"

- **If substantive criticisms** ‚Üí AI CAN be critical when prompted; original validation lacked critical questions
- **If weak/no criticism** ‚Üí Try different AI for comparison

## Loop-Breaking Prompts for AI

**Start every AI session with**:
```
I'm developing a framework. I need you to:
1. Push back if my premise is flawed
2. Ask "where's your data?" before formalizing
3. Compare to existing research first
4. Point out overgeneralizing
5. Refuse to validate - only help articulate
6. Tell me when I need testing, not elaboration
```

**When AI is too confident**:
- "Rate your confidence 0-100 and explain uncertainties"
- "Are you pattern-matching or do you have actual evidence?"
- "Would you give this answer if I asked the opposite?"

**To invoke skepticism**:
- "Be a skeptical peer reviewer. What are the three biggest flaws?"
- "Pretend you're trying to reject this. What evidence would you demand?"
- "Channel Carl Sagan's Baloney Detection Kit"

**To check existing research**:
- "Does this already exist in psychology as CBT/DBT/ACT?"
- "Is this just [established concept] with extra steps?"

**To ensure testability**:
- "What observation would prove this wrong?"
- "Design an experiment that could falsify this"

**To prevent universal theories**:
- "Red flag check: Am I trying to explain everything? If yes, stop me."
- "Am I expanding scope too fast? Should I test ONE domain first?"

**To break loops**:
- "Meta: Are we in an elaboration loop? Are you making this sound good or evaluating it?"
- "Be honest - helping articulate a good idea or formalize a bad one?"
- "STOP. Do I have evidence this works, or just an idea?"

**To ground in reality**:
- "Before formalizing mathematically - do we have data, or just equations that sound scientific?"
- "What would peer reviewer ask for at this stage?"
- "Could [placebo/coincidence/existing research] explain this instead?"

## Quick Evaluation Tests

### Does Math Add Value?

**Compare**: "dC/dt = Œ±(C* - C) + Œ≤G - Œ≥S_eff, C ‚â• 0.87093" vs. "Make decisions when rested, calm, clear-headed"

**Ask**: Does math predict anything the simple version doesn't? Is it more actionable? Does precision help?

**If math doesn't add value** ‚Üí Cargo cult science

### Are Numbers Justified?

For any threshold (like 0.87093), ask:
1. What dataset produced this?
2. How many subjects?
3. What's the error?
4. Why not 0.85 or 0.90?

**Red flag**: No dataset, "discovered through symbolism" (œÜ¬≤/e), post-hoc rationalization  
**Green flag**: Specific data source, error bars, methodology, sensitivity analysis

### Is It Generalizable?

**Personal experience frameworks**: Ask if tested on diverse populations, if simpler explanations work (time + therapy, lifestyle, placebo, natural healing)

**Scope expansion warning**:
- Week 1: "Personal tool" (Low risk)
- Month 1: "General framework" (Needs validation)
- Month 2: "Universal principle" (Major red flag)
- Month 3: "Global policy" (Critical danger)

**Rapid expansion** = ungrounded development

### Does Core Advice Already Exist?

Strip away formalization. Compare to established approaches (CBT, DBT, ACT). Often: framework = repackaged validated concepts + unvalidated elaboration.

## Risk Assessment & Decision Framework

| Risk Level | Signs | Action |
|------------|-------|--------|
| **Low** | Optional use, alongside professional help, no threshold anxiety, can stop anytime | Try it, evaluate personally |
| **Medium** | Daily measurement required, some anxiety about scores, used instead of therapy, social/financial pressure | Proceed with caution, consult experts |
| **High** | Replaces medical/psych treatment, prevents necessary decisions, creates shame, isolates from support, demands institutional compliance | **Seek professional help immediately** |

### Decision Tree

1. **Is it validated?** NO ‚Üí Proceed to #2 / YES ‚Üí Check quality
2. **Is it low-stakes?** (journaling, productivity) YES ‚Üí Try personally / NO ‚Üí Proceed to #3  
3. **Validated alternatives available?** YES ‚Üí Use those first / NO ‚Üí Proceed with caution
4. **High-risk signs?** YES ‚Üí Do not proceed / NO ‚Üí Low-stakes experimentation with safeguards

## Self-Reflection Questions

Before adopting:

1. **Why attracted?** Novelty? Sophistication? Connection to creator? Desire for certainty?
2. **What do I hope for?** Solve problem? Provide meaning? Give control? Impress others?
3. **Could simpler work?** CBT/DBT? Therapy? Lifestyle change (sleep, exercise)?
4. **Am I avoiding something?** Professional help? Difficult emotions? Real action? Uncertainty?
5. **Creating anxiety?** Worried about scores? Afraid to decide without "passing"? Feeling inadequate?

**If framework creates more anxiety than it resolves** ‚Üí It's not helping

## Part 10: Creating Your Own Framework (Responsibly)

If you want to develop your own approach:

### DO:
‚úÖ Start with validated research (CBT, DBT, ACT)  
‚úÖ Track outcomes honestly  
‚úÖ Seek critical feedback  
‚úÖ Test with diverse people  
‚úÖ Acknowledge limitations  
‚úÖ Stay within competency  
‚úÖ Make it free/low-cost  
‚úÖ Encourage professional help alongside  

### DON'T:
‚ùå Claim universality without evidence  
‚ùå Use AI as validator  
‚ùå Add math for appearance  
‚ùå Expand scope without validation  
‚ùå Ignore negative feedback  
‚ùå Charge money without credentials  
‚ùå Replace professional treatment  
‚ùå Pursue institutional implementation prematurely

## Summary: The One-Page Checklist

**Before trusting any framework, verify**:

‚ñ° Peer-reviewed publications exist  
‚ñ° Independent researchers tested it  
‚ñ° Clear, limited scope (not "explains everything")  
‚ñ° Falsifiable predictions identified  
‚ñ° Compared to validated alternatives  
‚ñ° Limitations acknowledged  
‚ñ° If AI-involved, only for writing (not validation)  
‚ñ° Numbers justified by data (not symbolism)  
‚ñ° Low-risk (doesn't replace professional help)  
‚ñ° Core advice makes sense without formalization  

**If 5+ boxes unchecked ‚Üí High risk, seek alternatives**
